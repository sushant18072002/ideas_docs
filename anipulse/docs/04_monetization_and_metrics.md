# 4. Monetization & Metrics (GTM)

## 4.1 Monetization Strategy

Building a mass-market B2C app requires careful unit economics to remain profitable while scaling.

### 4.1.1 The Free Tier (Ad-Supported)
*   **Native Ads (AdMob):** The primary revenue driver. We integrate Native Ads directly into the News Feed so they look identical to regular news cards (with a subtle "Sponsored" tag). This eliminates the disruption of pop-ups or banners.
*   **Affiliate Links:** "Where to watch" links (e.g., Crunchyroll, Amazon Prime Video) can be routed through affiliate networks (ShareASale, Impact) to generate commission if a user signs up for a streaming service via AniPulse.
*   **Merchandise Affiliates:** Occasional news feed drops for high-end anime figures (RightStuf, Good Smile Company) with affiliate tags.

### 4.1.2 AniPulse PRO ($1.99/mo or $14.99/yr)
To capture "whales" (highly dedicated users) who despise ads.
*   **Ad-Free Experience:** Removes all Native Ads.
*   **Customization:** "Waifu/Husbando" color themes. Custom app icon replacements.
*   **Offline Mode Plus:** Auto-downloads high-res cover art and trailers for offline subway viewing.
*   **Early Access:** Beta features for the upcoming "Social" phase.

## 4.2 Growth & Go-To-Market (GTM)

### 4.2.1 Social Media Engineering (The Viral Loop)
*   **TikTok & Reels (Organic):** Anime edits are one of the largest niches on TikTok. Partner with medium-sized editors (50k - 100k followers). Pay them $50 per video to include a 3-second outro: *"Track this anime schedule on AniPulse (Link in bio)."*
*   **Reddit Pre-Season Hype:** Two weeks before the start of a new Anime Season (e.g., Winter 2026), post a beautifully designed, high-res graphic on `r/anime` detailing the schedule. Watermark it heavily with "Generated by the AniPulse App."

### 4.2.2 ASO (App Store Optimization)
*   **Keywords:** Target high-intent, low-competition long-tail keywords. E.g., not just "anime", but "anime release calendar", "anime countdown", "anime schedule tracker".
*   **Screenshots:** Highlight the dark mode, the countdown timers, and the clean lack of clutter compared to competitors.

## 4.3 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

To measure if the BA and R&D assumptions are correct, we track the following strictly:

| Metric | Definition | Golden Target | Why it matters |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **D1 Retention** | Users who open the app 1 day after install | > 45% | Proves the app successfully solved their immediate need. |
| **D7 Retention** | Users who open the app 7 days later | > 25% | Proves the app is becoming a habit (checking the weekly schedule). |
| **Cost Per Install (CPI)**| Cost to acquire a user via paid TikTok ads | < $0.40 | Must remain extremely low for the Ad-Supported model to be profitable. |
| **Push Notification CTR** | % of users tapping an "Episode Dropped" alert | > 8% | The core heartbeat metric. High CTR = High trust in the app's usefulness. |
| **ARPU** | Average Revenue Per User (Monthly) | > $0.10 | Combined ad revenue + PRO subscriptions divided by total active users. |

## 4.4 Break-Even Analysis Projection
Assuming Cloudflare R2 + Supabase Free Tier = $0/mo server costs for the first 100,000 WAU.
*   **Monthly OPEX:** $99 (Apple Developer Account annualized) + $25 (Google Play) + $10 (Domain) = ~$20/month.
*   If we achieve a CPI of $0.35, to acquire 10,000 users costs $3,500.
*   If 10,000 users generate $0.05 ARPU via AdMob = $500/month.
*   The app hits financial break-even on user acquisition in 7 months, purely organically profiting thereafter.
