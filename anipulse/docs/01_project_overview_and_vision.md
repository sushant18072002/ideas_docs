# AniPulse: Project Overview & Vision

## Proposed Project Name: AniPulse
*Alternative Ideas:* AniDrop, SenpaiSync, NexusAnime, OtakuHub.
**Reasoning:** "AniPulse" suggests keeping a finger on the pulse of the anime world. It sounds modern, fast, and relevant to tracking schedules and news.

## The Core Product Vision
A dedicated, high-performance mobile application (Flutter) built for anime fans to track ongoing seasons, discover new releases, read anime-related news, and manage their watch schedules. The application aims to be lightweight, incredibly fast, and visually stunning.

## The Problem We Solve
Anime fans currently use multiple cluttered websites or bloated apps to track what's airing. 
1. Websites like MyAnimeList are data-rich but often feel dated, clunky on mobile, and slow.
2. Apps like Crunchyroll only track what's available *on their specific platform*.
3. Fans need a centralized, fast, and unified place to see *what* is dropping, *when*, and *where* they can watch it legally, regardless of the streaming service.

## Solution & Key Features
AniPulse provides a clean, unified dashboard for the modern anime watcher.

### MVP Features (Phase 1)
- **Daily Anime Calendar:** A visual weekly timeline showing precisely when new episodes drop.
- **Anime News Feed:** Curated news regarding season renewals, cast announcements, and trailers.
- **Discover & Search:** Search the database by genre, studio, season, or popularity.
- **Anime Details Page:** Trailers, synopses, episode lists, and "Where to Watch" links.

### Future Features (Phase 2 & 3)
- **My Watchlist:** Users can add anime to "Watching", "Plan to Watch", or "Completed".
- **Real-Time Push Notifications:** Alerts the moment a highly anticipated episode is marked as released.
- **Social Feeds:** User comments, review scores, and community discussions.

## Pros and Cons of Building This App

### Pros
- **Passionate Target Audience:** Anime fans are highly engaged and fiercely loyal to good tools. They actively seek out better tracking solutions.
- **Monetization Potential:** High potential for non-intrusive ads (like sponsored anime merch, VPNs) or a "Pro" subscription for ad-free tracking and custom themes.
- **Evergreen Content:** Every 3 months (Winter, Spring, Summer, Fall), a new anime season drops, naturally driving recurring user engagement.

### Cons & "What's Bad"
- **High Competition:** MyAnimeList (MAL) and AniList are massive giants. Getting users to switch their tracking ecosystem is difficult.
- **Data Maintenance:** Keeping airing details, news, and episode numbers accurate is a massive administrative undertaking if done manually.
- **No Direct Streaming:** Since we don't host the video content, users eventually leave the app to actually watch the episode. 

## Should We Do This?
**Yes, but with a specific angle.** 
To succeed against MAL and AniList, the app should NOT try to be a giant database initially. **It should focus purely on "The Pulse"**—providing the absolute best, most beautiful, and fastest UI for **daily schedules and breaking news**. If we excel at the "What's happening today" aspect, users will keep it installed right alongside their MAL app.
